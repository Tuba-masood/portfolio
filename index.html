Tell me about yourself?

I am an MS in Data Science candidate with a foundational background in Computer Science engineering and a passion for turning raw, complex data into actionable, real-world decisions. My technical journey spans building end-to-end analytical frameworks and developing full-stack solutions. For instance, I recently built a Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis framework to rank five major chronic disease programs for healthcare funding prioritization, using six normalized cost and outcome variables drawn from CDC, CMS, and AHRQ data. The model reproduced established clinical guidance, and I validated it against KNN and Decision Tree cross-checks while documenting its small-sample limitations honestly. My hands-on engineering toolkit is deeply rooted in Python, SQL, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and data visualization platforms like Tableau.

Beyond the technical skills, what truly defines my professional edge is my four-year background as a computer science lecturer. Teaching complex programming languages, algorithms, and data structures to diverse classrooms forced me to master the art of translation. Because of this experience, I don't just build intricate machine learning models or optimize SQL queries; I can confidently present the narrative behind the data to non-technical stakeholders, cross-functional engineering teams, and executive leadership alike. I thrive precisely at this intersection of deep analytical engineering and high-impact communication.

Currently, I am expanding my expertise through my Master's program at Fitchburg State University, aiming to graduate in June 2027. I am actively seeking Summer 2026 Data Science and Analytics internships, as well as full-time roles post-graduation, where I can deploy my blend of predictive modeling, software engineering, and storytelling. Ultimately, I am looking to join an innovative team where I can solve complex problems, build intelligent data pipelines, and help drive data-driven growth from day one.
